# Transition Notice: Retirement of Proprietary NDA in Favor of GNU GPLv3 Open Source

As of July 2026, the **BlurBubble (Stop Recording Me) Spatial Privacy Project** has officially transitioned from a closed-source, gated-development model to a **Public Open-Source GNU GPLv3 project**.

## 🔄 Why We Retired the NDA
Originally, the project was drafted under a highly restrictive Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (MNDA) and proprietary software terms to protect preliminary cryptographic specifications and Scottish Enterprise patent filing candidates. 

However, we realized that **privacy is a universal human right** and **everybody's responsibility**. True protection against unconsented biometric capture and spatial recording cannot succeed through secret agreements and proprietary gates. It requires:
1. **Public Collaboration**: Encouraging developers, legal counsels, school boards, and hardware engineers to openly inspect, build, and verify the safety of our protocols.
2. **Standard Interoperability**: Establishing an open, auditable, and decentralized reference spec that matches the draft IETF standard (RFC-9402) for smart-glasses and wearable beacons.
3. **Copyleft Protection**: Utilizing the strong copyleft protections of the **GNU GPLv3** to ensure that any major hardware manufacturer or software vendor who builds on top of our code is legally obligated to keep their modifications open and free for the community.

## 👩‍💻 How to Participate & Support the Project
Because we have removed all secret gates, you can now freely explore the interactive **Open Beta Sandbox**:
- **Test the Simulator**: Learn how spatial signal boundaries are drawn, adjust Kalman-filter parameters, and play with acoustic audio scramblers directly in the UI.
- **Review the Spec**: Check out the technical white paper and RFC-9402 proposal files.
- **Submit PRs**: We welcome contributions to improve our real-time blurring filters, add unit tests, or build mobile builds (CapacitorJS) for background signal emissions.

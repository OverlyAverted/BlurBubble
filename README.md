## 🔍 What is BlurBubble & How it Works (In Layman's Terms)

In the modern world, always-on smart glasses, security systems, action cameras, and drones can record your face, your kids, and your private belongings without your knowledge or consent. This data is often uploaded online, analyzed by facial-recognition companies, or used to train artificial intelligence models.

**BlurBubble puts the power of privacy back in your hands:**

1. **The Beacon (Your Shield)**: You carry a small, cheap physical tag (like a keychain, Pebblebee, or an AirTag) or run the BlurBubble app on your phone. This device silently broadcasts an encrypted, anonymous "opt-out" signal over short distances (about 25 meters).
2. **The Intercept (The Recording Device)**: When someone wearing compliant smart glasses or holding an action camera walks near you, their device detects your signal.
3. **The Mask (Instant Blurring)**: Before the camera can save any footage of you to its memory card or live-stream it to the web, the camera's internal processor applies an automatic, high-fidelity blur around your face, your children in school uniform, or your private screens. Your raw data is **never stored or uploaded**.
4. **The Net (Platform Sweep)**: For old-school spy cameras or non-compliant devices, our simulated web crawler actively scans public social media (TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube) and audio networks (Spotify, SoundCloud). It identifies unconsented exposures of your registered biometrics and issues automatic retroactive blurring or takedown compliance requests.
5. **Non-Intrusive & Selective**: BlurBubble NEVER jams, disables, or stops cameras from filming general surroundings. Anyone taking a photo or video at a concert, park, or tourist site captures the full background, scenery, and un-tagged bystanders in 100% crisp clarity. ONLY the specific opted-out person (or child wearing a tag) is blurred and voice-scrubbed.

## 🛠️ Technical Terms Explained Simply

- **BLE Beacons (Bluetooth Low Energy)**: This is a ultra-low-power radio signal commonly used by key finders (like AirTags). It runs for months on a single watch battery, silently whispering an anonymous identification number to nearby scanners without draining your phone or needing a cell connection.
- **Bluetooth 5.4 PAwR & Channel Sounding**: The latest Bluetooth standard. *PAwR* allows one beacon to send synchronized signals to multiple smart glasses nearby. *Channel Sounding* calculates the exact distance between you and the camera down to the inch, ensuring only people close by are blurred without slowing down the video.
- **Wi-Fi NAN (Neighbor Awareness Networking)**: A technology that lets nearby devices find and talk to each other directly over Wi-Fi without needing an internet connection or joining the same Wi-Fi router.
- **ECDSA (Elliptic Curve Digital Signature Algorithm)**: A highly secure mathematical formula used to sign messages. BlurBubble uses this to generate rotating digital signatures, proving you have opted out without revealing your real identity, name, or location. This prevents stalkers from tracking your beacon.
- **ISP (Image Signal Processor)**: The physical silicon chip inside smart glasses and phones that converts raw light from the camera lens into pixels. By applying the blur at the ISP level, the unblurred, raw image is **never saved to the device's hard drive or cache**.
- **Crawler Indexer**: An automated software robot that continuously browses public platforms (like Instagram, TikTok, and Spotify) to match and filter files. BlurBubble's simulated crawler checks for leaked face hashes or audio files and demands retroactive redactions.
